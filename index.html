CameraNoise: Learning Precise Camera Control with Video Diffusion in Noise Space

Abstract

In this work, we aim to control the camera pose for video diffusion. In previous approaches, we find that the numerical values of camera parameters cannot achieve precise camera control, even lead to artifacts in the generated contents. To address this, we propose CameraNoise, a temporally coherent stochastic representation warped from the camera intrinsic and extrinsic parameters. It is a reflection of camera pose in the noise space, independent of scene appearance, which enables diffusion models to achieve controllable video generation with respect to arbitrary visual content. Specifically, we propose a novel CameraNoise warping algorithm. With the help of the proposed CameraNoise, we achieve high-fidelity camera control in diffusion models with stable and high-quality videos.
